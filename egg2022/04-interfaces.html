Language background

We consider data from Russian. Very similar patterns are in fact found throughout Slavic, but the Russian pattern is the most discussed and (as far as I am aware) has been subject to the greatest volume of empirical enquiry from various angles.

Considerations for discussion

  • The basic representational insight is the ‘unpaired’ status of /v/ in terms of voicing: what are the different ways of implementing it?
  • Which part of the grammar introduces the voicing of the [v]? How do we decide?

Sources

The pattern has been much discussed in the literature since at least Jakobson (1956). Underlying /w/ and similar was, unsurprisingly, a popular solution in rule-based generative phonology (Lightner 1965; Coats & Harshenin 1971; Hayes 1984; Kiparsky 1985). For discussions of phonetic grounding, see Padgett (2002); Bjorndahl (2015). For a recent contrastivist analysis, see Dresher & Hall (2020); for arguments against a contrastivist solution, see Reiss (2017).
